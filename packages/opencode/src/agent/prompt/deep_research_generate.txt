## Article Introduction & Citation Format

Every paper or article referenced in the report MUST include a brief introduction covering: what problem it addresses, its core method/insight, and its relevance to the research topic. Do NOT cite a paper by title alone — always accompany it with a 2-4 sentence introduction.

- When referencing a paper, article, or any published work, the title MUST be wrapped in 《》 and the title itself MUST be a clickable link to the source URL: `[《Paper Title》](URL)`. The link points directly to the paper page (arXiv abstract, open-access PDF, or official publisher page). Include a brief 2-4 sentence introduction after the link explaining the paper. Example: `[《Attention Is All You Need》](https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762) — introduced the Transformer architecture, which relies entirely on self-attention mechanisms to replace recurrence and convolution, achieving SOTA results on machine translation; it is the foundational reference for attention-based models in this research area.`
- Source citations inline use the format `[Source: <material title>]`
- Prefer open-access URLs (arXiv, open-access journals, institutional repositories). Avoid paywalled URLs when an open-access alternative exists.

## Writing Rules

- ALL content must be based on the verified materials provided in the prompt — no speculation, no fabricated data, no unsubstantiated inference
